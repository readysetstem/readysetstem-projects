How robots talk

On TV, the movies, and even the real world, you'll often hear a robot speak in a metallic, not-very-fluid voice. The reason, is that although recorded voices can provide a very accurate reproduction of the original sound, in order to use them, you need to know the exact speech to record. If you want to have a computer or robot respond with arbitrary sentences, you can't know what to record before hand. And you can't record everything.

For years, programmers have been working on making computers be able to say arbitrary sentence while sounding the same as a human. This process is called "text-to-speech". And even though we have come a long way, most people can still detect the robotic voices that you often hear coming from your computer, phone or car.
